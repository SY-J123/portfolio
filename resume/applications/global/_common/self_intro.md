# Global Common Self-Introduction Base

Last updated: 2026-04-06

## 1. Why I Am Applying

I am interested in roles where product planning, operations, and cross-functional execution come together in a practical way. My background has taught me how to define ambiguous problems, align people around clear working standards, and improve execution speed without losing clarity. I am especially motivated by teams where strong communication and structured problem solving directly improve the quality of decisions and delivery.

## 2. Relevant Experience

My experience spans both live operations and product management. In operations roles, I monitored service metrics, coordinated with overseas branches and partners, and supported event and service execution under real business constraints. In product roles, I translated unclear requirements into structured flows, review criteria, and executable documentation that design and development teams could work from.

One of the clearest examples was introducing an AI-enabled documentation and prototyping workflow. I treated repeated review friction as a workflow problem, not just a documentation problem, and redesigned the process so stakeholders could review the same context more quickly. This reduced initial stakeholder involvement from 3 people to 1 and shortened reporting and approval lead time from 1 week to 3 days.

## 3. How I Work

I tend to work best in situations where there is ambiguity, cross-functional dependency, or repeated communication cost. I focus on making expectations, decisions, and review points explicit so teams can move faster with fewer misunderstandings. I value practical collaboration over process for its own sake, and I am comfortable working across product, operations, and external communication.

## 4. What I Can Contribute

If I join a new team, I aim to contribute by understanding the product and operating context quickly, identifying areas where decision-making or collaboration is slower than it should be, and turning those into clearer structures and more workable flows. I am also continuing to strengthen my data and statistics background so I can bring more evidence-based judgment into product and operational decisions.
