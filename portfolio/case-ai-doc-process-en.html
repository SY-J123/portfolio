AI-Driven Document Collaboration Process Improvement

LULU.AI · Dec 2025 - Mar 2026

I reframed document-heavy collaboration as a systems problem,
then connected PRDs, prototypes, and web publishing to speed up reporting and approvals.

Summary

A project that made document-based collaboration faster and easier to review.

Problem
Text-based sharing led to repeated reviews and decision-making loops.
Goal
Reduce communication overhead and time spent maintaining documents.
How
Connected prototypes, GitHub, and AI-assisted documentation workflows.
Outcome
Reduced reporting and approval lead time from one week to three days.

Background

In a fast-moving environment, product ideas were mostly shared through documents. But when screens and user flows also had to be explained, text alone was not enough to communicate the intent clearly. We needed a faster way to get everyone aligned.

Problem

Documents were being written, but text alone was not enough to communicate screens and user flows clearly. As a result, feedback and decisions went back and forth multiple times. In a fast-moving organization, that repeated communication and document work created real inefficiency.

  • Key points did not land in a single pass, so repeated explanations and confirmation were needed.
  • Different stakeholders used different interpretations, which made reviews and approvals take longer.
  • Too much time went into writing and revising documents, leaving less room for higher-value decisions.

Goals

  • Reduce the lead time for document review, reporting, and approval.
  • Help stakeholders work from the same context instead of interpreting things differently.
  • Shift the review process away from text-heavy explanation toward a more intuitive format.
  • Reduce communication costs caused by repeated explanation and reconfirmation.

Approach

1. I used prototypes instead of wireframes to communicate intent

Instead of relying on static wireframes, I chose a format that showed the actual flow. I built low-fidelity prototypes myself through vibe coding and shared screens and user flows together. In the documentation, rather than focusing on screen images, I organized the key information needed for review and implementation, such as states, data, and interaction flows.

2. I created a single point of reference for review

I published the prototypes to the web and managed both documents and outputs in GitHub, so stakeholders could always review against the same source of truth.

3. I designed a workflow that reduced documentation maintenance costs

I designed a workflow in which AI supported documentation maintenance, reducing the time spent writing and updating documents. That freed up more time for UX and higher-value product decisions.

What I Learned

This project taught me that collaboration is not about producing more documents. It is about designing a way of communicating that helps people understand the same context quickly. Prototypes that showed real flow were far more effective than static wireframes for alignment and decision-making. AI was not a substitute for product thinking, but a practical tool for reducing documentation maintenance and repetitive work so more time could go into UX and decisions.

Outcome

  • Reduced reporting and approval lead time from one week to three days.
  • Made it easier to align quickly around the same context by reviewing screens and flows together.
  • Lowered communication costs by reducing repeated explanation and reconfirmation.
  • Reduced time spent on document writing and updates, allowing more focus on higher-value UX decisions.
